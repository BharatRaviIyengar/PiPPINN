# Interpretable Variational Graph Autoencoder for Protein–Protein Interaction Prediction

## Objective

Develop an interpretable link prediction model that combines:

- Protein sequence information
- Graph topology
- Homology/congruence-based reasoning
- Uncertainty quantification via variational inference

The model predicts both:

1. Probability of interaction between proteins
2. Interaction strength/confidence

while providing biologically meaningful explanations for its predictions.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Data
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Protein Interaction Network

- Protein interactions obtained from STRING DB.
- Graph nodes represent proteins.
- Graph edges represent known protein-protein interactions.

### Node Features

Protein sequences are embedded using ESM2-3B.

For proteins within the ESM context limit:

- Per-residue embeddings are generated.
- Residue embeddings are averaged to obtain a single protein embedding.

For proteins longer than 1022 residues:

- Sliding-window inference is performed.
- Window embeddings are merged.
- Residue embeddings are averaged to obtain a single protein embedding.

Additional feature:

- Log-transformed protein length.

Protein length is scaled to match the magnitude of the ESM embedding dimensions and appended to the feature vector.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Variational Encoder
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The encoder consists of two complementary branches.

### 2.1 Raw Sequence Encoder

Input:

- Protein sequence embedding
- Protein length feature

Architecture:

- Multi-layer perceptron (MLP)

Outputs:

- μ_raw
- σ_raw

These parameterize a Gaussian latent representation describing sequence-derived information.

This branch captures:

- Sequence similarity
- Functional similarity
- Structural information encoded by ESM
- Protein-specific properties independent of graph topology

### 2.2 Neighborhood Encoder

Architecture:

- Two-hop GraphSAGE encoder
- Max-pooling aggregation

Outputs:

- μ_nbr
- σ_nbr

These parameterize a Gaussian latent representation describing graph-derived information.

This branch captures:

- Local network structure
- Functional neighborhoods
- Community membership
- Topological context

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Decoder Architecture
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The decoder combines multiple interpretable prediction mechanisms.

### 3.1 Congruence Decoder

Biological intuition:

If:

    Y interacts with Z

and

    X is sufficiently similar to Z

then

    X may also interact with Y.

This resembles homology-based transfer of interaction evidence.

Procedure:

For candidate interaction:

    X ↔ Y

all known neighbors Z of Y are examined.

For each neighbor:

- Similarity(X, Z) is computed.
- Neighbor edge strength(Y, Z) is incorporated.

A learned monotonic positive function converts:

    similarity(X, Z)

into an interaction probability contribution.

A second learned monotonic function combines:

    similarity(X, Z)
    edge_strength(Y, Z)

to estimate interaction strength.

Interpretability:

Predictions can be traced to specific neighboring proteins that provide supporting evidence.

### 3.2 Neighborhood Similarity Decoder

Biological intuition:

If:

    X interacts with Y
    Y interacts with Z

then

    X may also interact with Z.

This captures transitivity and neighborhood consistency.

Procedure:

- Compute similarity between neighborhood latent representations.
- Higher similarity implies greater likelihood of interaction.

Interpretability:

Predictions are explained through shared graph context.

### 3.3 Deep Decoder

A neural decoder learns interaction patterns that cannot be captured by the explicit congruence and neighborhood mechanisms.

Input:

- Raw sequence latents
- Neighborhood latents

Architecture:

- Deep MLP

Outputs:

- Interaction probability contribution
- Interaction strength contribution

Interpretability:

Represents residual predictive information not explained by explicit biological rules.

### 3.4 Final Prediction Layer

Final interaction probability is obtained through a generalized linear combination of:

- Congruence decoder output
- Neighborhood similarity output
- Deep decoder output

Final interaction strength is obtained from:

- Congruence-based strength prediction
- Deep decoder strength prediction

This allows explicit biological reasoning and learned nonlinear reasoning to contribute jointly.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Loss Functions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Variational Regularization

KL-divergence loss:

- Encourages latent distributions to remain close to the prior.
- Provides uncertainty-aware representations.

### Link Prediction Loss

Binary cross-entropy with logits.

Training uses a Positive-Unlabeled (PU) framework:

- Known interactions are treated as positives.
- Sampled non-edges are treated as uncertain negatives.

### Edge Strength Loss

Mean squared error (MSE).

Applied only to positive edges with known interaction strengths.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Data Processing and Sampling
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Train-Validation Split

80/20 split.

Additionally:

- Approximately 6% of edges are reserved such that both endpoint nodes occur only in the validation graph.

Purpose:

- Evaluate generalization to unseen proteins.

### Negative Edge Sampling

For each positive edge:

- Two random negative edges are generated.

Negative edges are sampled through random node pairing.

### Positive-Unlabeled Risk Weighting

Negative-edge risk is weighted inversely by the summed centrality of the endpoint nodes.

Rationale:

Highly connected proteins are better documented and are less likely to contain missing interactions.

Therefore:

- Negative edges involving highly connected proteins receive higher confidence (values near zero).
- Negative edges involving poorly connected proteins receive lower confidence (values near 0.5).

This accounts for incomplete network coverage.

### Edge Mini-Batch Sampling

Training edges are sampled using a hybrid strategy:

- 30% centrality-weighted sampling
- 70% uniform sampling

Purpose:

- Preserve network-core information.
- Maintain coverage of peripheral regions.
- Improve training stability.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Interpretability Strategy
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The model provides three complementary sources of evidence:

1. Congruence Evidence
   - Interaction inferred from similarity to known interaction partners.

2. Neighborhood Evidence
   - Interaction inferred from shared graph context.

3. Learned Evidence
   - Interaction inferred by the deep decoder.

Because these components remain separate throughout prediction, individual contributions can be inspected and analyzed independently.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. Potential Future Upgrade: Fourier-Based Protein Representations
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Current sequence embeddings are obtained through average pooling of per-residue ESM embeddings.

A potential enhancement is to replace or augment average pooling with a low-frequency Fourier representation of the residue embedding sequence.

Motivation:

- Preserve coarse positional structure.
- Reduce dilution of localized interaction signals.
- Improve congruence-based reasoning for multidomain proteins.
- Retain substantially more information than average pooling while remaining far more compact than residue-level representations.

Proposed workflow:

    Per-residue ESM embeddings
            ↓
    Dimensionality reduction
            ↓
    Fourier pooling across sequence position
            ↓
    Compact K × d representation

Expected benefit:

- Strongest impact on the congruence decoder.
- Moderate impact on the deep decoder.
- Limited impact on neighborhood-based predictions.

The Fourier representation would remain an optional future enhancement pending evaluation of memory, storage, and computational trade-offs.
